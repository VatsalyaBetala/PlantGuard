Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about PlantGuard's AI-powered plant disease detection platform.

How do I upload images for analysis?
Navigate to the Upload Images page and either drag & drop your plant images or click "Choose Files" to browse. You can upload multiple images at once. Our AI will analyze each image for disease detection within seconds.
What image formats are supported?
PlantGuard supports JPEG, JPG, PNG, and WEBP image formats. Each image can be up to 10MB in size. For best results, use clear, well-lit photos showing plant symptoms clearly.
Is my data stored securely?
Your uploaded images are stored temporarily for analysis and results display. You can delete all images anytime using the "Delete All Images" button in the Image Library. We prioritize your privacy and data security.
How does live recording work?
The live recording feature captures frames from your camera in real-time and analyzes them for plant diseases. Simply connect your camera, start recording, and our AI will process frames automatically for continuous monitoring.
How accurate is the AI detection?
Our AI models achieve 99.2% accuracy in plant disease detection, trained on millions of plant images. The system can identify over 50 different disease types across various plant species with high precision.
Is PlantGuard open source?
Yes! PlantGuard is completely open source. You can view the source code, contribute to development, or report issues on our GitHub repository. We believe in transparent, community-driven development.
Can I use PlantGuard on mobile devices?
Absolutely! PlantGuard works seamlessly on smartphones, tablets, and computers. No special app download required - just use your mobile browser to access all features including camera upload and live recording.
What information do I get from the analysis?
You receive detailed results including disease identification, severity assessment, AI explanation, and treatment recommendations. Visual heatmaps show exactly where diseases are detected on your plants.
