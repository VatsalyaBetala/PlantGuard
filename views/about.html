About

Plant pathology, accelerated by AI

PlantGuard is an open-source research platform that makes disease diagnosis faster, more transparent, and accessible to anyone working with plants — from smallholder farmers to academic labs.

Mission

Democratising plant disease detection

Most cutting-edge plant pathology tools live behind proprietary APIs or require specialist hardware. PlantGuard flips that: the full model stack, training pipeline, and UI are open-source, run offline, and work on a modest laptop or a Raspberry Pi.

We care about transparent predictions. Every diagnosis ships with a Grad-CAM heatmap so you can see exactly which regions drove the model's decision — no mystery verdicts, no vendor lock-in.

What's different

Designed for research use, not demo use

Six principles we optimise for — so PlantGuard is useful in a real lab workflow, not just a marketing reel.

Transfer-learned accuracy

ResNet-50 plant head + per-plant disease heads, fine-tuned on PlantVillage — not trained from scratch on a toy sample.

Fully open source

Every file — training, inference, UI — is in the public repo under the MIT License. Audit it, fork it, extend it.

Device-agnostic

Runs on CPU, CUDA, or Apple MPS. We've tested deployment to Raspberry Pi for field use.

Built-in explainability

Grad-CAM heatmaps attached to every prediction — not bolted on as an afterthought.

Pluggable backends

CNN and ViT adapters share the same interface via model_registry. Swap models with one env var.

Private by default

No telemetry, no outbound calls unless you explicitly use the LLM explanation feature. Images stay on your machine.

Stack

Technology under the hood

Boring, reliable, well-trodden tools. No hype-cycle dependencies we'll regret in six months.

Python & PyTorch

PyTorch 2.x for training, fine-tuning, and inference. torchvision handles the classical ResNet-50 backbone.

FastAPI & Uvicorn

A single FastAPI app serves the UI, exposes prediction endpoints, and streams Grad-CAM results — no microservice sprawl.

YOLOv8

Ultralytics YOLOv8n crops the leaf from the uploaded image before classification — dramatically improves on-background noise.

Grad-CAM

Hand-rolled Grad-CAM targeting the last conv block (layer4[-1]) of ResNet-50, producing overlay heatmaps.
